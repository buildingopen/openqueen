# OpenQueen Launch — Content Ideas

Ranked by virality potential. Each idea is independent — pick and combine.

---

## 1. WhatsApp Screenshot Post
**Score: 9/10**

The single highest-leverage asset. A real screenshot of:
- Incoming: "fix the auth bug in payments.py"
- Outgoing: "✅ Done. Bug fixed in payments.py:42. Tests pass. 1 commit."

No explanation needed. The image IS the post. One tweet with this image and a one-liner caption will outperform any thread without media.

**Effort:** Low — you already have OpenQueen running.
**What's needed:** Take the screenshot. Clean up the chat if needed (hide other messages).
**Caption:** "My new coworker works while I sleep."

---

## 2. 30-Second Screen Recording
**Score: 9.5/10**

Record:
1. You type a task in WhatsApp on your phone (show the phone screen)
2. Terminal on the server showing Claude working (agent.py output)
3. WhatsApp notification arriving on your phone: "Done."

No narration. No cuts. Just the loop. Post to Twitter, LinkedIn, and LinkedIn video (separate upload).

**Effort:** Medium — needs screen recording setup and a clean terminal.
**What's needed:** OBS or QuickTime on Mac, phone screen mirroring (AirPlay or similar).
**Note:** This is the highest-ROI thing you can do for launch. Everything else is secondary.

---

## 3. "AI agent while you sleep" angle
**Score: 8/10**

Frame it around a specific use case: you queued 3 tasks before bed, woke up to 3 Done notifications. Very relatable for developers who work across timezones or have a day job.

**Effort:** Low — write from experience.
**Draft hook:** "I queued 3 tasks before bed. Woke up to 3 Done notifications. AI agents don't sleep."

---

## 4. Technical deep-dive thread
**Score: 6.5/10**

Explain the architecture: why Gemini as orchestrator, why WhatsApp as the UI, how Done When conditions work, how it handles parallel projects. Good for credibility and SEO, bad for virality. Post this as a follow-up 3–5 days after the launch post gains traction.

**Effort:** Medium.
**Audience:** Engineers, not general tech.

---

## 5. "The commute use case" story
**Score: 7/10**

Personal story: you were on a train/plane/bus, sent a task from your phone, arrived and the work was done. Works well on LinkedIn. Very relatable for remote workers and founders.

**Effort:** Low — write it from a real experience.
**Draft hook:** "I sent a WhatsApp message from the airport. By the time I landed, Claude had fixed the bug, written the tests, and committed the changes."

---

## 6. Open source drop post
**Score: 6/10**

Clean "shipped it" post. Link to GitHub, brief description, invite contributors. Works well in open source communities (Hacker News, Reddit r/programming, dev.to) more than social media. Submit to HN Show HN the same day as the Twitter launch.

**Effort:** Very low.
**Draft title for HN:** "Show HN: OpenQueen — coding agent you control from WhatsApp"

---

## 7. "Built in public" angle
**Score: 6.5/10**

Document the build: what broke, what surprised you, what you'd do differently. Works on Twitter as a retrospective thread. Builds trust with technical audience.

**Effort:** High.
**Best posted:** 1–2 weeks after launch, once you have usage data to reference.

---

## 8. Comparison post: "vs babysitting Claude Code"
**Score: 7.5/10**

Side-by-side: what it looks like to babysit an agent (terminal, waiting, context-switching) vs OpenQueen (send message, get notification, done). Could be a diagram, screenshot split, or just text.

**Effort:** Medium.
**Hook:** "Before vs after adding OpenQueen to my workflow."
